Jeremy Christisen

Data Warehouse Maven

Jeremy is quiet and private which can make him difficult to get to know. Professionally, he is a hard-working data warehouse expert who strives for perfection and gives his all; personally he is a smart, kind, loyal, respectful, non-judgmental, open-minded, humble, fun soul. Some of his interests include running, hiking, chess, poker, movies, monkeys, meditation, and activities that are intellectually challenging. 

Originally from Iowa, Jeremy moved to the Lansing area in 1999 when he began with Bull. He has spent the last 16 ½ years supporting MDHHS with all things data warehouse related. Jeremy has had various titles throughout the years - ‘Coding Monkey’ being his self-proclaimed. He made Dan McCandless’ vision of Eligible Months a reality, engineered the Generic Load Process used by developers for data warehouse jobs, designed the CHAMPS data warehouse…which are just a few of his accomplishments. 

He will be greatly missed by all who have worked with him and while saddened to see him we are thankful for all of his contributions and wish him the best.
